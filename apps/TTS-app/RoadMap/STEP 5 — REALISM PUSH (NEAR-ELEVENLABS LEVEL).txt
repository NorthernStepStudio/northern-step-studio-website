STEP 5 GOAL

Make speech that has:

Natural rhythm

Emotional motion (not static emotion)

Human imperfection

Physicality (breath, mouth, pauses)

If you do only half of this, you’re already top-tier.

1️⃣ Emotion ≠ Static (Animate It)

Humans don’t speak with one emotion value.

Emotion Curve Over Time
emotion(t) = base + wobble + intent


Implementation:

def emotion_curve(t, base, intensity=0.15):
    return base + intensity * np.sin(2*np.pi*t/3)


Apply per-phoneme or per-sentence.

🎯 Result: speech “moves”.

2️⃣ Prosody Prediction (HUGE)

Prosody = timing, pitch, stress.

Even simple heuristics work:

Sentence-level

Question → rising pitch

Statement → falling pitch

Emphasis → longer vowel

if text.endswith("?"):
    pitch_curve += np.linspace(0, 0.2, n_frames)

Word Emphasis (Cheap but Effective)

Mark emphasis via:

CAPS

punctuation

SSML-style tags

<I>really</I> important


Then:

+20% energy

+10% duration

3️⃣ Micro-Timing Jitter (Secret Sauce)

Perfect timing sounds robotic.

duration *= np.random.normal(1.0, 0.04)


Apply to:

phoneme length

pauses

syllable spacing

⚠️ Keep it subtle (3–6%).

4️⃣ Breath & Mouth Sounds (Massive Perceptual Gain)

Add:

inhale before long sentences

lip noise on plosives

subtle exhale after emotion peaks

Breath injection
if sentence_length > 12:
    audio = inhale + audio


Breath volume:

tied to emotion energy

lower for calm

louder for excitement

5️⃣ Loudness & Spectral Shaping

Humans don’t speak at constant loudness.

RMS normalization with drift
rms *= np.random.uniform(0.9, 1.05)


Add gentle:

low-freq warmth (150–300 Hz)

high-freq roll-off for calm voices

6️⃣ Imperfection Modeling (DO THIS LAST)

Add controlled flaws:

Slight pitch cracks

Breath noise floor

Tiny clipping on emphasis

audio += 0.002 * np.random.randn(len(audio))


⚠️ Too much = uncanny.
Less than you think.

7️⃣ Voice Personality Layer (Optional but 🔥)

Each voice gets:

{
  "tempo_bias": -0.05,
  "pitch_instability": 0.03,
  "breathiness": 0.4,
  "emotional_reactivity": 0.8
}


Now voices feel distinct, not just different pitch.
